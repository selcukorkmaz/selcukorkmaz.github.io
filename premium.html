Academic Profile

Biostatistics, trustworthy machine learning, and research software for biomedical science.

I develop open-source R software and methodological work for reproducible biomedical data science. My research focuses on leakage-aware model evaluation, transparent statistical workflows, and the reliability of machine learning in clinical and biological datasets.

Research Position

A methodological and software-led academic practice.

My work sits at the intersection of biostatistics, biomedical machine learning, and open-source scientific software. I am especially interested in how evaluation pipelines, data leakage, class imbalance, and reporting quality shape the credibility of computational findings.

  • Trustworthy machine learning Principled evaluation under limited, imbalanced, or clinically complex datasets.
  • Research software Reusable R tools for data access, model auditing, and transparent workflows.
  • Reproducibility and rigor Clear reporting, defensible methodology, and auditable end-to-end analysis.
